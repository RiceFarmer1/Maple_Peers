  • Although everyone who immigrates to a new country needs time to adjust, youths often struggle more than others. As new student immigrants, it could be difficult for you to socialize with others, make friends, succeed in school, or get involved in your community. If any of these concerns are applicable to you, then you have found the right place. At Maple Peers, we assemble a team of students who were once newcomers just like you. They endured through the tough circumstances and overcame challenges such as the language barrier, education, and integrating into the community. Now, they are here to guide you through your journey and share their own experiences.

    Our Group

    Here, anyone can register to either become a mentor or a mentee. If you are a new immigrant, you can apply to be a mentee, and we will pair you up with a suitable mentor. If you want to volunteer, you can apply to be a mentor and help provide support to mentees. Before you sign up for any clubs or mentorship oppourtunities, join our WhatsApp community by scanning the QR code below!

    Volunteering

    Volunteering is a great way to get involved in your local community. In Ontario, you need to aquire at least 40 volunteer hours in order to recieve your high school diploma.




  • English Club

    At the English club, we teach basic reading, writing, and speaking skills to a group of immigrant youths. We also offer support for homework assignments, projects, or essays.



    Coding Club

    At the Coding Club, we teach the fundamentals of programming in Python and potentially other languages in the future. We will assist you with your own projects as well.




    Drawing Club

    At the Drawing Club, we offer a chance for students to explore art and the various methods of drawing. We will help you create the best artworks possible.



    Contest Math Club

    At the Contest Math Club, we provide training for University of Waterloo's CEMC math contests from grade 9 to grade 12, including the CIMC, CSMC, Euclid, and more.
